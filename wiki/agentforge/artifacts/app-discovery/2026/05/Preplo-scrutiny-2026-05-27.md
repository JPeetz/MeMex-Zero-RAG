### Preplo's 3 Critical Vulnerabilities (Attack Vectors)

1. **iOS-only — Android gap is existential.** 70% of global mobile users on Android. Gen Z foodies in Asia, Latin America, Africa — massive recipe-video consumption markets — cannot use Preplo. ReciMe already ships on both. **Whoever launches a quality Android social recipe extractor first owns this market.**

2. **No meal planning, no community, no Pinterest** — Preplo is a single-purpose extraction tool with gamification, not an ecosystem. Users extract a recipe, cook it, and leave. There's no retention loop beyond cooking streaks (which require cooking, a 30-60 minute activity). ReciMe's community and meal planning keep users in the app daily.

3. **1 free extraction is hostile onboarding.** The user converts from "this looks interesting" to "I hit a paywall" in 3 minutes. No collection behavior develops (unlike ReciMe's 5-recipe free tier, which builds switching cost). A competing app offering 10-20 free saves would capture price-sensitive users immediately.

### 3 Table-Stakes Features Preplo MUST Add (within 90 days):

2. **Meal planning** — currently "coming soon" but no timeline. This is the bridge from "I cooked one recipe" to "I cook 4 recipes this week."
3. **Pinterest + Facebook import** — ReciMe supports these. Every missing platform is a user who sees a recipe they can't save in Preplo.

## Section 5: X-Factors

### #1 Churn Reason & Cancellation Intercept
- **Predicted #1 churn reason:** "I only cook a few recipes a month — $9.99 is too much for occasional use."
- **Intercept:** Offer "Pause Plan — keep your recipe library, billing resumes when you extract again" at $2.99/mo pause fee. This saves 20-30% of casual cook cancellations.
- **Secondary intercept:** Downgrade to Annual at $49.99 before canceling completely.

### Regulatory/Legal Risks
- **LOW.** Recipe extraction from public videos is fair use / equivalent to link sharing.
- **Moderate copyright risk:** Extracting full recipes from creators without attribution. Must add "Recipe extracted from @creator on [platform]" attribution on every recipe card. This is legal protection AND a growth channel (creators want attribution).
- **Caching strategy:** Preplo caches processed videos so same clip isn't analyzed twice — this is smart for cost but could be attacked as copyright hosting if full transcripts are stored. Keep cached data as structured recipe data, not raw video/transcript.

### The Shipyard Advantage (Non-Replicable Moat)
Preplo's RevenueCat Shipyard win with Eitan Bernath is **not replicable by competitors.** It means:
- Built-in influencer distribution from the creator who defined the category brief
- RevenueCat's platform integration (subscription + analytics) from day 1, not bolted on
- PR + press + industry credibility that a cold-start app can't match
- The Shipyard program selected 7 winners from 900+ — this is a stronger signal than "top 10 on Product Hunt"

**Counterpoint:** Shipyard apps historically have strong launches but poor long-term retention. The creator partnership drives downloads but not necessarily product quality. Preplo must execute on the Android + meal planning + community roadmap or the Shipyard advantage decays.
