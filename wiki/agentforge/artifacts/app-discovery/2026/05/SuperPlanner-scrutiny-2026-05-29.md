# Super Planner ADHD — Competitor Scrutiny Report

**Date:** 2026-05-29 | **Pipeline:** App Discovery v4.0 | **Scaffold Winner:** ThoughtLatch

---

## Section 1: Product Overview

- **Name:** Super Planner: ADHD Routine AI
- **Platform:** Android only (Google Play)
- **Status:** Live on Google Play
- **Category:** Productivity / Task Management
- **Price:** Free + In-App Purchases (pricing unlisted on Play Store listing)
- **Developer:** Individual/small team

Super Planner ADHD explicitly targets the ADHD market with AI-powered voice input, task breakdown, mood check-ins, and personality-customizable AI tones. From their Play Store listing: "With AI powered voice input, you can create tasks in seconds, and break them down into actionable steps easily."

---

## Section 2: Feature Deep-Dive

**Advertised Features:**
- AI-powered voice input: Speak → tasks created + broken into steps
- Smart Breakdown: "Let AI turn your big goals into manageable, step-by-step actions, perfect for ADHD brains"
- Mood Check-in: Daily mood logging with "fortune cookie" motivational messages
- AI Personality Toggle: Switch between "humorous" and "encouraging" AI tones
- Daily routines: Morning/evening routine planning
- Task management: Standard to-do list functionality

**Strengths (vs ThoughtLatch):**
- ADHD-specific positioning is excellent — name + description + features all ADHD-targeted
- AI breakdown of tasks into steps is genuinely useful for executive function
- Mood check-in is a unique differentiator — bridges task management and mental health
- AI personality toggle is clever — ADHD users respond well to personality and gamification
- First to Android in the voice-first ADHD space

**Weaknesses (vs ThoughtLatch):**
- **No iOS version** — misses 60%+ of the ADHD paid-app market (iOS users over-index for subscriptions)
- Play Store review count appears low (<100) — early stage
- No on-device AI claims — likely cloud-based, raising privacy concerns for ADHD users logging personal thoughts
- No calendar sync mentioned
- No widget support
- No voice tone/urgency detection
- No thought stream / capture paradigm — it's a planner, not a capture tool. Different use case.
- No data export
- No shareable cards or viral features
- Mood check-in + fortune cookies may feel gimmicky to some users — splitting focus from the core voice capture value

---

## Section 3: Competitive Position

**Strengths:**
1. **Best ADHD positioning on Android** — most directly competitive product to ThoughtLatch's value prop
2. **Fun UX** — mood check-ins + AI personality toggle make the app feel engaging, not clinical
3. **AI task breakdown** — genuinely addresses ADHD executive function pain
4. **Android-first** — ThoughtLatch will launch simultaneously, but Super Planner has Android head start

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Planner, not a capture tool** — Super Planner wants you to plan your day. ThoughtLatch just wants to catch your thoughts. The ADHD user needs BOTH, but the capture-first paradigm is more aligned with "I had a thought but it vanished" — the primary pain point.
2. **No iOS** — massive market gap. If ThoughtLatch launches iOS first, it owns that half of the market.
3. **Low review count** — suggests early stage or low adoption. Not yet scaling.
4. **Cloud AI privacy risk** — ADHD users logging personal thoughts + moods into a cloud-based AI raises data privacy concerns. ThoughtLatch's on-device-only processing is a direct counter.

---

## Section 4: Market Position Analysis

**Google Play Traction:** Live but low review count. No visible growth curve.

**Threat Level to ThoughtLatch: MEDIUM**
- Most credible competitive threat in the ADHD voice-first space
- Android-first gives them a platform head start
- ADHD-specific positioning is well-executed
- BUT: No iOS, low traction, privacy concerns, and planner-first (not capture-first) paradigm create vulnerability windows

**What Would Raise Threat Level to HIGH:**
- iOS launch announcement
- App crosses 1,000 reviews on Play Store
- On-device AI processing announced
- Funding round or acquisition by a larger productivity company
- Partnership with ADHD coaching/therapy platforms

---

## Section 5: Overtake Recommendations

1. **Capture-first, not planner-first.** Super Planner wants you to manage your whole life. ThoughtLatch does ONE thing: catch the thought before it vanishes. This is the sharper value prop for the "I forget my ideas" ADHD pain.
2. **iOS-first launch.** Super Planner is Android-only. Launching iOS first captures the higher-monetization half of the market with zero direct competition.
3. **On-device privacy as a weapon.** If Super Planner uses cloud AI, ThoughtLatch's on-device processing becomes a trust signal. "Your thoughts never leave your phone." This matters to ADHD users who already face stigma.
4. **Out-feature on capture, not planning.** Super Planner has AI task breakdown + mood tracking. ThoughtLatch should have better CAPTURE: voice tone detection, instant transcription, auto-categorization. Let Super Planner be the planner. Be the catcher.
5. **Reddit community engagement.** r/ADHD has 1.9M members. Launch there with an authentic story. Super Planner doesn't appear to have Reddit presence.

---

## Section 6: Vulnerability Windows

- **Now:** Android is uncontested by ThoughtLatch. Launching Android within 2 weeks of iOS captures that platform.
- **If Super Planner launches iOS:** They become the cross-platform leader in ADHD voice-first. ThoughtLatch MUST ship iOS first and fast.
- **If Super Planner adds on-device AI:** Their privacy weakness closes. Speed matters.
