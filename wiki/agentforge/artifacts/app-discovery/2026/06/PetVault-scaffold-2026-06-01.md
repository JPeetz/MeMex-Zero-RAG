## PROBLEM

Pet owners manage medical records across paper files, email attachments, multiple vet clinics, and insurance portals. Reddit threads reveal universal pain:
- "Vet records keep having inconsistencies" (r/Pets)
- "Vet won't provide my pet's records when I request them" (r/dogs)
- "Why does every pet insurance ask for full medical records?" (r/PetAdvice)
- "I wish my vet clinic would offer payment plans" (r/Pets)
- Current workarounds: dedicated email address per pet, Dropbox folders, paper binders, Google Keep

## KILLER DIFFERENTIATORS

1. **AI Receipt & Record Scanner** — Photograph vet bills, vaccine cards, lab results. AI extracts dates, vaccine types, dosages, costs. Structured data from unstructured paper. No competitor has this.
2. **Insurance-Ready Export** — One-tap PDF export formatted for every major pet insurer (Fetch, Trupanion, Nationwide, Healthy Paws). Solves the #1 pet insurance complaint.
3. **Multi-Vet Sync** — Records from different clinics merged into single timeline. No vet portal dependency.
4. **On-Device AI** — All processing local (CoreML/MediaPipe). Zero pet health data leaves device. Privacy moat.
