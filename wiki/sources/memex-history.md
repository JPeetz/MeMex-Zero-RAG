# The Memex: Bush's 1945 Vision

Vannevar Bush's seminal 1945 essay "As We May Think" introduced the Memex concept — a precursor to personal knowledge management systems and hypertext.

## The Original Vision

Bush proposed "a device in which an individual stores all his books, records, and communications, and which is mechanized so that it may be consulted with exceeding speed and flexibility." [Source: raw/memex-history.md]

## Key Innovation: Associative Trails

Human memory works by association, not alphabetical indexing. The Memex would allow users to create "trails" linking related information:

> "The process of tying two items together is the important thing... thereafter, at any time, when one of these items is in view, the other can be instantly recalled."

[Source: raw/memex-history.md]

## Why It Matters Today

The web became something different — public, chaotic, search-engine-organized. Personal wikis are closer to Bush's vision:

- Private, curated collection
- User-defined associations ([[associative-trails]])
- Connections as valuable as documents

[Source: raw/memex-history.md]

## The Missing Piece (Solved in 2026)

Bush couldn't solve maintenance. Who builds and maintains the trails? In 1945, it required tedious manual work.

In 2026, the answer: **LLMs**. They don't get bored, don't forget cross-references, and process documents faster than any human. [Source: raw/memex-history.md]
