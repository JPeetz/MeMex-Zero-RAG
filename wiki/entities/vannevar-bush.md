# Vannevar Bush

American engineer and science administrator who conceived the Memex in 1945, anticipating personal knowledge management systems and hypertext by decades.

## Background

- Director, Office of Scientific Research and Development (WWII)
- Oversaw Manhattan Project coordination
- Dean of Engineering at MIT
- Founded Raytheon

[Source: wiki/sources/memex-history.md]

## The Memex (1945)

In "As We May Think" (The Atlantic, July 1945), Bush described a hypothetical device for personal knowledge management:

- Store all books, records, communications
- Mechanized for speed and flexibility
- Create [[associative-trails]] between documents

The key insight: human memory works by **association**, not alphabetical indexing. [Source: raw/memex-history.md]

## Legacy

- Influenced Ted Nelson (hypertext)
- Influenced Doug Engelbart (mouse, collaborative computing)
- Cited by Tim Berners-Lee (World Wide Web)
- Referenced by [[andrej-karpathy]] in LLM Wiki

## The Unsolved Problem

Bush couldn't solve **maintenance** — who builds and maintains the trails? This remained unsolved until LLMs made automated knowledge curation possible. [Source: wiki/sources/memex-history.md]
